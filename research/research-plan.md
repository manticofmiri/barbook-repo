# Research Plan — BAR.BOOK

## Objective

Understand how people currently discover and book gastronomic venues, what frustrates them, and what they need from a dedicated app — with a particular focus on users with dietary restrictions and group coordinators.

---

## Research Questions

1. How do people currently find restaurants or cafés when going out with a group?
2. What tools or platforms do they use, and what are their limitations?
3. How important is dietary information (vegan, vegetarian, gluten-free) in the decision-making process?
4. What role does table reservation play in the dining-out experience?
5. What information do users want to see before choosing a venue?
6. What payment methods do users prefer?

---

## Methodology

**Semi-structured interviews** — 8 participants, 30–45 minutes each.

**Screener criteria:**
- Goes out to eat at least once a month
- Has used Google Maps, social media, or a similar platform to find restaurants
- Mix of dietary profiles (omnivore, vegetarian, vegan)
- Ages 22–45

---

## Interview Script

### Introduction

*"Hello [name], thank you so much for joining me today. My name is Miriam and I work in User Experience. What we do is constantly talk to users to understand their experiences — what they enjoy, what frustrates them, and what they need. This will be a relaxed conversation, so please feel completely comfortable."*

*"I'll be recording this session, but everything you share is confidential. We'll only use the recording to review answers later so we don't miss anything. Everything you tell us helps us learn and improve — so please feel free to say whatever comes to mind."*

*"Any questions before we begin?"*

---

### Warm-Up — Profile Validation

- What's your name? Where are you from? How old are you? What do you do for work?

### Topic 1 — Current Behaviour

- Have you ever searched for a restaurant or café on Google or social media?
- Have you ever made a reservation at a restaurant online?
- Tell me about the last time you did something like that — what was it like?
- What kinds of things are most important to you when choosing a venue?
- What payment methods do you usually use?

### Topic 2 — Pain Points

- Have you ever had trouble finding a place that met everyone's needs in a group?
- How do you handle situations where some people in your group have dietary restrictions?
- Is there anything about current apps or platforms that frustrates you?

### Topic 3 — Needs & Expectations

- If there was a perfect app for finding and reserving restaurants, what would it do?
- Are there any features you've seen in other apps that you really liked?

---

## Usability Test Protocol

### Scenario

> *"Your birthday is coming up and you want to celebrate with your friends. You'd really love to have afternoon tea or coffee at a venue with outdoor seating. Several of your friends are vegan and don't always find places with a full plant-based menu."*

### Tasks

| # | Task | Success Criterion |
|---|------|-------------------|
| 1 | Search, filter, and reserve a table for 8 people for your birthday tea-time | Reaches reservation confirmation screen |
| 2 | Log in to the app | Successfully logs in |
| 3 | Save a venue to favourites | Venue appears in saved list |
| 4 | View your existing reservations | Navigates to reservations section |

### Key Metrics

- Task completion rate
- Time on task
- Errors and backtracking
- Think-aloud observations
- Post-task satisfaction (1–5 scale)

---

## Closing Questions

- Were there any moments where you felt stuck or confused?
- Is there anything you would change?
- Was there anything that worked particularly well?
- Was there anything you expected to find in a certain place that wasn't there?

## Affinity Map Themes

After synthesising interview data, insights were clustered into these themes:

| Theme | Key Insight |
|-------|-------------|
| **Dietary discovery** | Vegan/vegetarian users pre-filter before any exploration — exclusion is their baseline fear |
| **Group coordination** | One person bears the burden of finding a "safe" venue for everyone |
| **Trust signals** | Users trust platforms with real photos, up-to-date hours, and confirmed menus |
| **Outdoor spaces** | Outdoor seating is a primary differentiator, but no platform surfaces it well |
| **Booking simplicity** | Calling to check availability breaks the experience — in-app confirmation is essential |
| **Return visits** | Saved lists drive repeat bookings — memory of good places is the biggest retention hook |
